College admission interviews are your chance to stand out from thousands of applicants. The key is not just what you say, but how confidently and authentically you present yourself.

## Tip 1: Research The Institution

Before walking into any interview room, study the university's mission statement, notable alumni, and recent achievements. Interviewers can immediately sense when a candidate has done genuine homework versus surface-level browsing.

## Tip 2: Practice Your Personal Narrative

Craft a compelling 2-minute story about who you are, what drives you, and why this particular institution aligns with your vision. Practice it until it feels natural—not rehearsed.

## Tip 3: Prepare for Behavioural Questions

"Tell me about a time you failed" is not a trick question. It's an invitation to demonstrate self-awareness and growth mindset. Structure your answers using the STAR framework (Situation, Task, Action, Result).

## Tip 4: Ask Thoughtful Questions

Always have 2-3 questions prepared for the interviewer. This signals genuine interest and intellectual curiosity—two traits every admissions board values deeply.

## Tip 5: Dress Appropriately

First impressions matter. Dress one level above what you think is appropriate. When in doubt, business casual is always a safe choice.
